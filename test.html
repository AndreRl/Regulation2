CHAPTER 1. GENERAL ORDERS
Subsection 1. Purpose
The uniforms of USDF are issued under direction of the Secretary of Defense (SecDef) and carry the force of a standing order. The uniforms provide a distinctive form of dress that allows personnel to represent USDF and perform their duties with recognition and prestige. Furthermore, the various uniforms identify Habbos as USDF personnel and, more specifically, members of their individual military branches. The USDF uniform must be worn with dignity and pride and should never be used to discriminate or cause offense. The uniform does not represent the wearer; rather, the uniform represents USDF, an organization that desires to uphold a distinguished service record.

Subsection 2. Enforcement
This regulation defines the composition of USDF's authorized uniforms. The uniform must always be worn on USDF (excludes external R&R), allied, and foreign grounds (personnel who possess VIP privileges are exempt). Furthermore, the uniforms are distinctive visual evidence of the authority and responsibility vested in the wearer by the SecDef. The prescribing authority determines when and where it is appropriate to wear the uniforms discussed in this regulation. All components of the uniform must be worn as described. The USDF's personnel must present a proud and professional appearance that will reflect positively on the organization. Any procedures or components regarding uniforms, mottos, grooming, etc., that are not discussed in these regulations are prohibited.

Subsection 3. Temporary Alterations to the Uniform
  • O1+ personnel may authorize the temporary alteration and non-regulation of one accessory item (eyewear, headwear, etc.) for one hour at a time.
  • JSD+ personnel may authorize the temporary alteration and non-regulation of clothing items (shirt, pants, etc) and accessories.
  • Only NSC+ personnel may authorize the temporary alteration of the entire uniform with choice by the personnel (otherwise known as Non-Uniform Hours).
  • Personnel are not permitted to wear unauthorized accessories or uniforms that have office membership or FEATs restrictions, such as specific colors and types of accessory pertaining to these conditions.
  • Temporary uniform alteration hours may not be hosted back to back with previous hours unless approved by a member of the National Security Council (NSC).

    •  
In part with keeping tradition in USDF, senior command (JSD+) members have occasionally had their own "random event/uniform accessory" items in HQ. The permission is granted executively through the Secretary of Defense, in part with keeping tradition from the original viewpoint of USDF's founder Mr. Sycron. His view was to keep HQ fun, enjoyable and inclusive for all. We do not want HQ to be overly strict or have an environment in which personnel do not wish to return.

Subsection 4. Behavior while in Uniform
While in uniform, it is inappropriate and detracts from military smartness for personnel to walk from point to point whilst acting inappropriately; this includes, but is not limited to, language or behavior that is explicitly sexual, racist, foul, or offensive to others. This includes the use of language that is generally not accepted by all social standards and cultures.

Subsection 5. Officers on Leave
Officers on an approved leave must dress in professional civilian attire while in the HQ. Refer to Chapter 6, Subsection 3 for more information. Furthermore, they must modify their motto by adding "OL" to the very beginning of it.

CHAPTER 3. MOTTO REGULATIONS
Subsection 1. General Regulations
  • All personnel must wear the correct motto at all times while on USDF premises or when representing the USDF.
  • Personnel do not wear promotion tags. Be sure to check the PTS prior to letting someone through sentry.
  • All FEAT tags are written within {brackets}. Furthermore, Commissioned Officers and civilians with {5} are not required to display their FEATs in their motto. However, any other personnel with or without {5} must display all FEATs as mentioned on the PTS.
  • Ranks are separated from offices by a forward slash (/).
  • Enlisted personnel (E1-E3) and Commissioned Officers (O1+) are permitted, though are not required, to write their rank out in full.
  • All O7+ personnel are permitted to display Habbo stars at the very end of their mottos (alt + 157).
  • Civilians utilize "[USDF]" in their motto in lieu of a branch.
  • Civilians do not display their GS level in their mottos.
  • Retired personnel who qualify to enter the HQ (see Regulation 4 for details) must display their motto as such for entry: [US Branch] Ret. Rank
    Example: [US Navy] Ret. CDR

Subsection 3. Civilian Dress
  • Civilians have no set uniform. However, they are expected to dress business casual at the minimum to present a professional, respectable, and realistic appearance.
    • Personnel with the name colored gold on PTS and with the motto format "[USDF] Position" are considered civilians.

      •  
  • Members of OSD+ (excluding Branch Chiefs) may wear civilian or military dress, even if they are not a civilian.
  • NSC+ may direct civilians to correct their clothing as necessary.
  • Civilians are subject to Chapter 4. Grooming Standards & Accessories as well as Chapter 5. Cover Regulations and are authorized to wear medals.
  • Colors must remain appropriate and realistic to business casual attire, all clothing and accessories included.
  • Wearing a hat with a suit is not permitted.
  • Personnel in civilian-designated positions with acting status (denoted by A/ on PTS and in the motto) may not wear civilian dress until fully appointed.
  • Fully appointed civilians may still wear the military uniform options presented below in line with regulation.
